Has PSG ruined the football transfers by signimg Neymar and Mbappe for 200 mn?

Technically the transfer of Zidane back in 2001 was a lot more controversial because it was more than half of the team’s revenue. Getting Mbappe on loan with a future buyout is something that happens quite often in other clubs. It’s a way to borrow money from the future and is a standard way of doing business in the football world. English clubs do it all the time in order to get the transfer done before the transfer window shuts, Italian clubs do it because they want to borrow money from the future.
PSG (and Manchester City to some extent) are exploiting the loopholes available in the FFP. They did it a few years back and were fined 60 million Euros (IIRC) and didn’t flinch an eyelid about it (they also had to compete in the Champions League with a 21-man squad instead of a 25-man squad). What will probably happen again is that they’ll be given another fine, that will be promptly paid by their owners using their pocket change.
They haven’t ruined the transfer system though. Chinese/Middle Eastern clubs are doing the same (but they’re too far away for us to care). They’re only paying a slightly inflated price for Neymar if you take into account the marketing benefits he will bring to the club and what other clubs like Real Madrid were willing to pay for Mbappe. Sure they have brought a lot of money into football, but this money will trickle down the bottom of the football pyramid. In the end of the day, the transfer of one player, no matter who he is, doesn’t break the system.

Why hasn't democracy been working in the Middle East so far in so many countries?

The people with money in their pockets get what they want while the common people who were given so much attention during the election campaign are now ignored, too bad so sad. What I’m seeing are systems made up of liars who would do anything just to get re-elected, they would sell their soul to the devil just to secure that chair of theirs. Entire groups are marginalized as a form of fear tactic to get people to vote for a certain individual or party like sheep.

Well I could but good luck getting them to win. Why would I want a system which further strengthens oligarchs and screws me; the citizen, over. Let me use an example closer to home.
Kuwait, my country. The most democratic or second most in the Arab World. The elected parliament that has legislative powers has served the wealthy merchants more so than any single group of people. No and even if they didn’t win the rights to it, they would none the less try very hard to ruin it for the other sponsor, ruining it for the rest of the country in the process. Democracy is making us fall behind in important areas like infrastructure, in either case the average person has no power so can I get the amazing infrastructure please?
Then there is the “dividing” factor. A system which relies on the marginalization of the other or dividing society is not welcomed. Democrat or Republican? Tory or Labour? More of an identity than political opinion. If we had democracy in the Western fashion every tribe, sect, and social class would have a party. Society is divided enough thank you very much. You know that German democracy is actual democracy because it actually brought change to Germany, in America institutions are too large for a change in the head to matter because if Donald Trump actually had any de facto power that mattered I assure you, America would be on the verge of collapse but he doesn’t.
Indeed, it was the facade of democracy that brought us such tyrants as Jamal Abdulnasser, Saddam Hussein, Ali Abdullah Saleh, Hafez Al Assad and his son among others. It is these countries that attempted to install a Western system of government on their lands that are the most corrupt, least educated, and wriddled with poverty. You have your own flawed political system and we have our own flawed political system. Every political system is flawed one way or the other, there is not one perfect system built for all.

Without systems we develop on our own based on our experiences you get strongman rule found in the Arab Republics.
Democracy is not the perfect system as people have been brought up to think. The Western system is not the perfect system. Our system isn’t perfect either. If we go enforce our system on you, it won’t work; just as your system won’t work for us if you attempt to enforce it on us. Our societies are different and were formed differently. That doesn’t make either of us superior to the other, but it does make us different.
